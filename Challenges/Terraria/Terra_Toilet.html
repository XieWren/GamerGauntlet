Rules

Fresh world, fresh equipment

"Seriously? THIS is what you used the Broken Hero Sword for?"

Usually, a Broken Hero Sword is used to craft the Terra Blade, one of the materials for the Zenith, the most powerful weapon in the game. However, in the Terraria 1.4 Journey's End update, the Broken Hero Sword gained a new use; to be crafted into the Terra Toilet. Very quickly, this became a meme speedrun category for Terraria enthusiasts.

In this guide however, we will not be teaching how to speedrun the game, but instead give a guide on how this can be achieved.

Some steps have been thrown in to make the game more manageable for the average player, to ensure the gameplay remains fun and interesting for them throughout the playthrough. Have fun.

Things to know

  • The crafting recipe for the Terra Toilet requires a toilet and a Broken Hero Sword

  • Broken Hero Swords have a 25% (43.75% in higher difficulties) chance of dropping from Mothrons, which only spawn during a Solar Eclipse after all 3 mechanical bosses have been defeated (The Twins, Destroyer and Skeletron Prime)

  • The 3 Mech Bosses are only spawnable with materials in a hardmode world, so Wall of Flesh has to be defeated.


Guide (Optimal Route)

    Getting Started

  1. Upon first spawning in, quickly build a couple houses for NPCs (5 is a good number).

  2. While waiting, go underground to look for the following:

    • Life Crystals
    • Materials for a Furnace and Anvil
    • Mobility Accessories such as a Cloud in a Bottle and Hermes Boots
    • Gems for a Gem Hook
    • Bombs to get the demolitionist to spawn

    Anvils can sometimes be found in Underground Cabins.

    Skeleton Merchants sell bombs, so if you find him, sell whatever loot you don't need, and buy at least a stack of 99. Though they are slower than dynamite, you can only get them from the demolitionist, and bombs are more cost efficient anyways.

  3. After getting what you need, head to the Underground Jungle. You will want to be looking for the Boomstick as its probably the best item you can get at the moment.

    If you don't trust your abilities, the Musket/Undertaker from Shadow Orbs/Crimson Hearts is a weaker alternative.

  4. After achieving Boomstick, return to base and sell everything you don't need, and get some dynamite from the Demolitionist and more bullets from the Arms Dealer(if they spawn).

    5. Preparations for Hardmode

  5. Start using bombs/dynamite to make a hellevator down to the Underworld.

  6. Using the blocks acquired on the way, prepare a bridge to travel on while fighting the Wall of Flesh.

  7. If a Voodoo Demon spawns while building the bridge, good for you! If none spawn, building a house for the guide in the Underworld, then killing him with lava is an alternative to getting the Wall of Flesh to spawn.

  8. The plan for killing Wall of Flesh is to lay down dynamite on the bridge for the Wall of Flesh to 'float' into. However, that may be difficult (without armour) for the average player to do.

    Alternatively, the Space Gun with Meteor Armour is a strong pairing due to Space Gun not costing any mana, but may be more time-consuming.

    After killing the Wall of Flesh, Hardmode begins. To mine hardmode ore, the Molten Pickaxe is needed, so bombing Hellstone is needed (though crafting the armour is optional, as Tier 1 hardmode ore is easily better.)

    9. Hardmode

  9. Do the normal Hardmode ore grind, being

    1. Destroy Demon/Crimson Altars with the Pwnhammer
    2. Mine Cobalt/Palladium
    3. Craft a Cobalt/Palladium Pickaxe/Drill
    4. Mine Mythril/Orichalcum
    5. Craft a Mythril/Orichalcum Anvil

    Unless one is exceedingly lucky, finding all 3 mech boss spawners is not feasible. Luckily, they can be crafted at the Mythril/Orichalcum Anvil.

    Finding the Third Tier of Hardmode Ore is not required, of which the reason will become evident later on, as the Titanium/Adamantite Forges are not required.

    10. The Mech Bosses

  10. The Destroyer is the first mech boss to take out. After gathering enough Souls of Night (Underground Corruption/Crimson), craft a Mechanical Worm to be spawned at night.

    • If you are alright with cheesing the boss, build a 2 by 3 room at least 100 blocks high (to be safe, 150 blocks), with platforms to stand on. This prevents the Destroyer from even reaching you. Drop dynamite down at the Destroyer. As the probes Destroyer spawns die from the explosions the moment they spawn, they should not be a problem. If all goes well, it should die before 4:30am.
    • A less cheesy method of taking out Destroyer, would be the Daedalus Stormbow - Holy Arrow combination. However, if you prefer a challenge, the Mythril or Orichalcum Repeater could be used instead, though the Titanium/Adamantite Forge will be required, and a good reforge may be needed for Destroyer to be taken out before 4:30am.

  11. Destroyer, upon defeat, should drop Hallowed Bars. Using the Mythril/Orichalcum Anvil, the best time-efficient weapon is the Hallowed Repeater, which will be the primary weapon for the rest of the run.

  12. To get a Mechanical Skull for Skeletron Prime, bones are needed from the Dungeon. That means defeating Skeletron, which should be easy with Hardmode gear. If you are lucky, there should still be time before the night ends to do so, allowing us entry to the Dungeon before the end of the night.

  13. Upon getting the bones needed, if nightime is still some time away, pay a visit to the Jungle Temple.

    14. Suddenly Jungle Temple

  14. (No Major Glitches) To find the Temple, dig/bomb straight downwards in the Jungle in intervals of around 60-100 blocks. An easy way to get into the temple without the Temple Key is to place three platforms in front of the door, hammer them downwards and walk through while holding down. Once in, grab 2-3 Solar Tablets worth of fragments, and return to base.

  15. Alternatively, standing at the entrance (in front of natural Lizahrd Brick Wall) allows Lizahrds and Flying Snakes to spawn in the area. As each enemy has a 1 in 7 chance to drop 1-2 Solar Tablet Fragments, it is a possible alternative to fighting Plantera for access to the Temple.

    16. ...Back to Mech Bosses!

  16. If it is nightime and the Jungle Temple has not been found, prepare for battle with the other two mechanical bosses (Twins and Skeletron Prime). Purchase a jetpack from the steampunker (if arrived) and craft the Toilet part of the Terra Toilet. If you have beaten the game once already, you should be familiar with how the bosses work and be able to survive consistently. For the arena, a simple platform around 50 blocks above the ground will do.

    17. Plantera?

  17. Classic Plantera Strategy: Dig a giant box arena with layers of platforms to navigate around the Bulb. When fighting Plantera, fly around it in a big circle to avoid her projectiles. Plantera's Thorn Balls should fall to the bottom of the arena.

    In Phase 2, the arena is a lot tighter, and Plantera's Tentacles are more likely to stunlock the player without Knockback immunity. If you are unable to beat Plantera, consider getting the Cobalt Shield while in the Dungeon.

    18. The Eclipse

  18. Players should have enough Hallowed Bars to craft at least 2 pieces of Hallowed Armour after both The Twins and Skeletron Prime are defeated. Once daytime arrives, summon the Solar Eclipse using the Solar Tablets, and wait for a Mothron to spawn.

  19. After surviving long enough for a Mothron to spawn, killing the Mothron without getting killed, and getting the Broken Hero Sword to drop, head back to the Anvil to craft the Terra Toilet. Sit on it, and the speedrun/adventure is complete!


Variations

Expert Mode (Starting to Suffer)

  • Starting exploration is more dangerous due to increased enemy damage, especially in the Underground Jungle

  • Wall of Flesh is faster, so dynamite timing will differ.

  • Destroyer, if using Mythril/Orichalcum gear, may be a lot more difficult to defeat by 4:30am. Consider using Adamantite/Titanium gear, or the Daedalus Stormbow.

  • Solar Eclipse enemies are more likely to kill you near the end. The moment an enemy banner is dropped, consider putting it up to reduce enemy damage and defence.

Master Mode (Massive Luck Required)

  • I am not sure if this is considered a speedrun anymore. You are better off playing the game normally, unless you can no-hit bosses for durations 3 times longer than in Classic Mode, especially without armour.

Journey Mode (Easy)

  • On the opposite end of the spectrun, there is Journey Mode. While the game is easier, speedrunning is more difficult as the timing is more tight; even split second decisions make a decision

< id="Actual_Speedrun">Actual Speedrun (Could be Worse)

  • Honestly, there's probably a set seed that makes everything easier, but I wouldn't know. If you are speedrunning with a set seed, it would be a good idea to load up a world with the same seed, and look at it through TEdit beforehand to have an idea of locations such as the Jungle Temple and Dungeon.

  • RNG is also important, as you might get unlucky with enemy spawns and get killed

  • It should also be noted that you would need to defeat every boss with possibly lacklustre equipment. That means it would be a good idea to practice beforehand to ensure bosses can be consistently beat.

Calamity Auric Toilet% (Starting to Suffer)
