Rules

Fresh world, fresh equipment

"You better have a lot of free time on your hands, because this is going to take a long, long time."

Ahh, movement keys. Something all of us use while playing Terraria. Even I am using them while typing this out. Now, imagine not being able to use them. That's tough. It's like trying to operate a computer without a mouse, or a television without a remote.

However, just like all great companies, design is an aspect Re-Logic is obviously talented at. It's like how a television always has manual controls that can be used even without a remote. It's like how the Tab key allows one to navigate around options on a computer even without a mouse. If you don't expect these kinds of situations, and create a fail-safe, then you simply aren't a good designer of products/games.

Now that we have gotten that out of the way, why not let's test out this fail-safe that Re-Logic created? I am sure it will be fun!

(P.S. Re-Logic did not in actuality create a fail-safe, its just a coincidence that it's possible; also my analogy was a stretch and a half, but let's not talk about that. Instead, let's both pretend you never saw this.)

Things to know

  • So, what do no movement keys mean? Well, we are talking about the arrow keys and the buttons W, A, S and D. Any button other than that is fair game.

  • That means that movement options such as walking or dashing, climbing and navigating platforms, are not allowed. Any other movements are fair game.

  • For more information on game controls, see here. It should help make things clearer.


Guide

    Getting Started

  1. Unbind movement keys to prevent accidental usage. Yes, this is a thing to ensure you never make any mistakes and accidentally press WASD.

  2. Now, we want to generate a world with trees around, so that we can minimally craft a Work Bench. If you want, you can wait for Guidey to aggro some slimes, which will hopefully attack YOU instead (cause why not), so you can poke them with the Copper Shortsword for their gel to make torches.

  3. So what should we do, now that we basically cannot move? Well, we can always dig straight down. We are looking for a Hook, which we can use to navigate around, without the use of the WASD buttons. We could try to get a hook and chains for the standard Grappling Hook, but grinding without movement keys is horrible. Instead, we are going to get a Gem Hook, which can be crafted using 15 of any type of gem.

  4. However, we need some mobility to be able to mine them. This can be easily solved after you mine enough iron/lead ore to craft minecart tracks, or if you stumble on some by accident. If the tracks are in your range, you will be able to 'teleport' to them.

  5. At the same time, you may face trouble while mining straight down, such as falling into a pit full of water, and drowning, or not falling into a pit full of water, and breaking your knees. If you block the top of the lake after digging 3 blocks below it, we can jump up every now and then to replenish air supply. As for the pit, we can actually place 2 layers of blocks below us. If we mine the top layer, we can add new blocks below the second layer. Rinse and repeat to get all the way to the bottom, slowly but surely.

  6. After getting the hook, you can finally get some important things that don't seem to make any sense at first. These include:

    • Hermes Boots for mobility
    • A minimum of 20 Gold/Platinum Ore, in increasing increments of 20
    • At least 1 Ruby, though you will probably find more

    After getting enough items, proceed to commit die to return to the surface. Craft all your ores into the various items you need, as well as a couple Gold/Platinum Crowns, and prepare for the Mr. Royal Blue.

    7. Boss 1: King Slime

  7. To craft a Slime Crown, you will need to bring a Crown and 20 Gel to a Demon/Crimson Altar. While most spawn in the Corruption/Crimson, some spawn in the Underground Level, which you may be able to find with less diffculty.

  8. Prepare a bridge made of blocks near Space, but without spawning any Harpies. This will be helpful for Hardmode later. Note that platforms are not being used, as grappling to them gets the player stuck in the platforms unless they jumped and lose momentum, which may not be ideal.

  9. Anyways, spawn King Slime, and simply by utilising the ancient art of 'get away from your enemy', you can more or less take out King Slime. A good weapon to have would probably be a bow with Flaming/Frostburn Arrows; you don't really need much to take out the weakest boss in the game.

    10. Aftermath

  10. What we do want though, is the drops. King Slime drops the Solidifier, which allows us to craft Slime Blocks out of Gel. However, what's important is that combining Slime Blocks with Ice Blocks creates Frozen Slime Blocks. When moving on these blocks, you maintain 100% of your momentum, which is very useful to retain the Grappling Hook's velocity if you let go while being pulled in.

    11. Meteor Armour

  11. If you got some bombs through exploring, the Demolitionist should have spawned. Grab some explosives, take a visit to the Corruption/Crimson, and blow up 1 or 2 Shadow Orb/Crimson Heart (Destroying a third will spawn the Eater of Worlds or Brain of Cthulhu). A meteor should spawn somewhere in the world not long after.

  12. The amazing synergy between the Meteor Armor and Space Gun is more than likely to carry you through the rest of Pre-Hardmode. Just make sure not to die to the Meteor Heads!

    13. The unremarkable bosses

  13. That being said, with speed that is difficult to reach even with Hardmode Gear, bosses such as Eye of Cthulhu, Brain of Cthulhu, and Eater of Worlds easily fall in your path. They are not very remarkable to talk about, not requiring much strategy, especially in Classic Mode.

  14. Queen Bee and Skeletron, however, will require a few changes in strategy. With Queen Bee, it is a good idea to get a Bezoar beforehand, for Poison Immunity. With Skeletron, make sure to switch direction before the Head Spin attack, as you can easily get stunlocked if you run out of platform space.

    15. Wall of Flesh
    Preparations

  15. If you feel the Space Gun isn't cutting it and need to change up your weapons, good weapons to use are:

    • Melee: Enchanted Sword, Starfury, Sunfury
    • Ranger: Molten Fury, Hellwing Bow, Pheonix Blaster, Beenades
    • Mage: Magic Missile, Flamelash, Demon Scythe, Water Bolt
    • Summoner: Vampire Frog Staff(which jump and hit multiple times), Hornet Staff, Imp Staff

  16. If you need more gel for Slime Blocks for the hellbridge, you can farm King Slime as he drops more gel than is needed to summon him. Alternatively, with the mechanic and a timer, one can rig up Slime Statues that spawn in lava, and leave them for a while and get a snack.

    17. The Fight

  17. Nothing to it. You can move faster than the Wall of Flesh all the time. Make sure to slow down once in a while so that the Wall is on screen for you to hit. If you have enough bridge, you can definitely win.

    18. The Hardmode Grind

  18. Do the normal Hardmode ore grind, being

    1. Destroy Demon/Crimson Altars with the Pwnhammer
    2. Mine Cobalt/Palladium
    3. Craft a Cobalt/Palladium Pickaxe/Drill
    4. Mine Mythril/Orichalcum
    5. Craft a Mythril/Orichalcum Anvil
    6. Mine Adamantite/Titanium
    7. Craft Adamantite/Titanium Forge

  19. In the list of possible Armour sets you can use, good options include:

    • Frost Armour, because of the helpful Frostburn debuff it provides
    • Forbidden Armour, if you choose to play magic/summoner
    • Palladium Armour, because of the high health regeneration
    • Titanium/Adamantite Armour, if you are lazy and don't want to farm
    • Spider Armour, as an easy armour set to acquire that increases survivability while you try to get the other sets

  20. You can also get a pair of wings (Frozen Wings being the current best), a Worm/Tendon/Illuminant Hook, and the Ankh Shield.

    21. The Mech Bosses
    Preparations

  21. It is incredibly unlikely that spawners for all the Mech Bosses will drop during grinding. This means the following items will have to be gathered (for 1 round of boss fights):

    • 15 Iron or Lead Bars
    • 9 Souls of Light
    • 9 Souls of Night
    • 6 Rotten Chunks or Vertebrae
    • 3 Lenses
    • 30 Bones

  22. This will require fighting and defeating Skeletron, as access to the Dungeon is needed to collect Bones; if you have not done so yet, now is the best time to do so. With Hardmode Gear, Skeletron should be easy to defeat.

    23. The Fight

  23. Remember when Daedalus Stormbow got nerfed in 1.4? Yeah, me neither. The combination with Holy arrows still absolutely shred the Mech Bosses. In case you forgot, they drop from Hallowed Mimics with a 25% drop chance. If you are worried, test it out on Skeletron first.

  24. Against The Destroyer, make sure it cannot reach your platforms as you will definitely have a difficult time moving around, especially without WASD keys.

  25. Against Skeletron Prime, try not to move too fast, unless Prime uses its Head Spin attack. Target the Laser Arm, and you are good to take him out after that.

  26. Against The Twins, try to move in a single direction, and only turn when you have to. The Twins will require more vertical mobility than the other Mech Bosses, and as such it's a really good idea to set up columns of platforms in mid-air that can be grappled to to move sideways if needed.

    27. Plantera

  27. The steampunker should have spawned in by now. Set up two teleporters in the Underground Jungle, with the Plantera Bulb in the middle. When Plantera spawns, teleport between the two teleporters with the help of Pressure Plates and a low ceiling to avoid Plantera's attacks. Try to close off the space to prevent Plantera' ranged attacks from hitting you, while ensuring you can still attack the Plant.

  28. Alternatively, with a set of Turtle Armour, Fetid Baghnakhs and some health potions, one can stand in place and win a DPS race against Plantera. For better survivability (in case you are worried), the following items can be considered:

      Potions
    • Ironskin Potions
    • Endurance Potions
    • Lifeforce Potions
    • Rage Potions
    • Wrath Potions
    • Thorns Potions
    • Flask of Ichor
    • Items that grant the Well Fed Buff and its upgrades

      • Accessories
    • Moon Charm and its upgrades
    • Moon Stone and its upgrades
    • Cross Necklace and its upgrades
    • Frozen Turtle Shell
    • Worm Scarf (if in Expert Mode)
    • Brain of Confusion (if in Expert Mode)
    • Titan Glove and its upgrades
    • Feral Claws and its upgrades
    • Warrior Emblem
    • Avenger Emblem

      • Others
    • Bast Statue
    • Sharpening Station
    • Dryad's Blessing

  29. If this feels too cheesy, set up two layers of frozen slime blocks, with the top layer around 15 blocks to the left/right of the bottom layer, as well as one extra long corridor of frozen slime blocks.

  30. In the first phase, circle around Plantera using the two layers of platforms, dodging any of her Thorn Balls.

  31. In the second phase, head down the long corridor of Frozen Slime, as Plantera will try to charge you instead.

    32. Golem

  32. For Golem, Holy Arrows with Frostburn is still really viable, as long as you upgrade to a Clorophyte Shotbow.

  33. Alternatively, get Spectre armour with Hood to facetank, or Turtle Armour and Vampire Knives if you have Crimson.

  34. (Though honestly, do you really need a guide on beating Golem?) Okay if you do, add a layer of Frozen Slime Blocks on the floor, hammering them when there are slopes. If you want, you can place Teleporters on either side so that you can maintain mobility.

  35. Trap Golem in a corner using blocks (starting around 7-10 blocks above the ground), then attack its knees.

    36. Martian Madness & The Frost Moon
    Math

  36. If you need help understanding this part, a simple mathematical equation should prove helpful:

    Spectre Hood = Broken
    Razorpine = Broken
    Spectre Armour + Razorpine = Insanely Broken

    37. Martian Madness

  37. But before that, we will need something to help us actually get the Razorpine in the first place. That would be the Laser Machinegun, which is definitely not a weaker Razorpine.

  38. Seeing that the Martian Saucer is most likely always faster than you, a good method to fight it would be to use Teleporters again. Before the event, set up two teleporters the same way you did Plantera, but in a room with Background Walls (to prevent spawns).

  39. Using Summons or Sentries, one can take out the Martian Saucer while avoiding its attacks via the teleporters.

    40. Frost Moon

  40. If you are lazy and don't want to do Martian Madness first, you can. It may be more difficult to defeat Everscreams, though.

  41. Consider setting up an arena in the air, with a ceiling and lava on the floor. Grounded enemies can't reach you, while the Elf Copters can't shoot through the ceiling. Everscream however, will be able to pass through blocks to reach the player; so long as the arena is small enough, you essentially only have to deal with spawning Everscreams.

  42. With a bit of time and luck, the Razorpine is yours. Heck, while taking the trouble, why not get the Blizzard Staff as well? It's essentially the group attacking weapon (as opposed to high single-target DPS).

    43. Lunatic Cultist & The Pillars

  43. With good enough armour (you know, like the one we just talked about), face tank with even a bit of dodging is more than enough on Classic Mode.

  44. Otherwise, if you are lazy to grind two extra events, the Twins Arena is really good for both vertical and horizontal mobility. Just make sure to always be there to hit the Cultist when he splits, as the Phantasm Dragon, like many worm-based enemies, will mess you up without the help of WASD.

  45. After beating the Cultist, Pillar Time! Every pillar has a different strategy to increase survivability (Remember, it doesn't matter if you die, because every enemy defeated is progress! It's alright to spawn several graveyard biomes, that's very natural.)

  46. To improve survivability on all pillars, going into its range and killing a few enemies at a time, while slow, is a great way to ensure not dying.

    47. Solar Pillar

  47. Good luck! That is, unless you have a proper arena around the area utilising hammered frozen slime blocks that slope upwards for vertical mobility (while avoiding the Crawltipedes).

  48. Technically, it is possible to build them beforehand by putting 4 of them at regular intervals around the map, to increase the chances of the Solar Pillar spawning in the vicinity of one.

    49. Vortex Pillar

  49. Stay in a 3 by 2 hole and fire Area of Effect / Homing weapons outwards, as most enemies cannot hit you effectively unless they get into or above the hole.

  50. When attacking the Vortex Pillar, try to always be at a different height to prevent getting shot.

    51. Nebula Pillar

  51. Always stay moving, to prevent Brain Sucklers in the Nebula Pillar's range from reaching you, and usually avoid homing enemy attacks (Looking at you, Evolution Beast).

    52. Stardust Pillar

  52. Farm Star Cells outside of Stardust Pillar range, then get in and finish the job quickly.

    53. Moon Lord

  53. Moon Lord Time! If you can't make it to a nearby arena in time, there's always manual spawning with the Celestial Sigil.

  54. Weapon Choices:

    • Melee:
      Solar Eruption, Terra Blade, Influx Waver, Possessed Hatchet, North Pole, Daybreak, The Eye of Cthulhu, Flairon

    • Ranger:
      Phantasm, Tsunami, Eventide, Aerial Bane, Vortex Beater, Xenopopper, Chain Gun

    • Mage:
      Blizzard Staff, Razorpine, Stellar Tune, Razorblade Typhoon, Nightglow, Betsy's Wrath, Laser Machinegun, Nebula Blaze, Nebula Arcanum

    • Summoner:
      Terraprisma, Stardust Dragon Staff, Xeno Staff, Sanguine Staff, Stardust Cell Staff, Raven Staff, Kaleidoscope, Morning Star, Dark Harvest

  55. Honestly, Moon Lord is pretty cheese-proof without minecarts or asphalt. Add to that 1.4's gimmick of letting Phantasmal deathray pass through blocks, and you have a bit of a problem.

  56. This means teleporters are the way to go. Set up two teleporters close enough to each other such that Moon Lord doesn't teleport to you as well, but far enough to avoid getting hit by the Phantasmal Deathray on the other side. Include pressure plates and a low ceiling like in the Plantera fight.

  57. It is a good idea to flood the teleporters with Honey, and to include Campfires and Heart Lanterns. Try to fit housing for the Nurse and the Dryad if needed.

  58. Buff up, get the Celestial Sigil (or wait for impending doom). Try to take out all 3 True Eyes at the same time, and with luck, you will have completed your Terraria no WASD challenge!


Variations

Expert Mode (Starting to Suffer)

  • It is very likely that you will face a lot more difficulty in fighting bosses, and may be forced to cheese some bosses (Moon Lord being the most likely candidate, in before you get the Rod of Discord).

  • However, as long as you can dodge well enough, the strategies mentioned for the other bosses should be good enough to win the game.

Master Mode (Massive Luck Required)

  • Um... Look. Just commit cheese, okay? There is legitimately no way you can beat these bosses unless you try an ungodly amount of times, and honestly, why would you want to suffer this much?

Calamity Classic Mode (Starting to Suffer)

  • Given the sheer difficulty of the Calamity Mod, it's really quite surprising that the Slime God Lore Item can save your life. It helps you maintain your speed as if you are always sliding, so with a Hook that gives fast enough acceleration, you are basically good to go!

  • Being Calamity however, you probably will have to take a couple of tries to beat every single boss though.

  • If trying the challenge out on Expert Mode, you will just need to dodge better. On Revengeance and Death Mode, however, the extreme changes in boss AI may be bad for your mental health. So uh, maybe don't try it?
